# CHECKLIST_REVISE.md
## Paragraph and argument-level revision pass
*Use this file after a full draft exists. Work section by section, not sentence by sentence.*

---

## R1 · Paragraph Structure

- [ ] Every paragraph has a topic sentence that states its claim and positions it as an extension of the previous paragraph
- [ ] Topic sentence is not a setup phrase — it makes the point, doesn't announce it
- [ ] The paragraph's body supports the topic sentence with concrete evidence, named mechanisms, or specific examples
- [ ] No paragraph ends without completing the logical move it opened
- [ ] No two consecutive paragraphs open with the same grammatical subject or same construction

---

## R2 · Transitions & Logical Flow

- [ ] Every paragraph-to-paragraph transition carries an explicit logical relationship (extension, contrast, qualification, consequence)
- [ ] No transition that just restates the previous paragraph's conclusion
- [ ] No "in this light," "in this context," or similar mid-sentence stalling transitions
- [ ] Transitions between sections name what changed, not just that something changed

---

## R3 · Argument Architecture

- [ ] Each major claim cluster has at least one serious rival reading addressed
- [ ] Claims are bounded to what the evidence demonstrates — no sweeping systemic claims from single events
- [ ] Observation, inference, and speculation are clearly separated — not smoothed over
- [ ] No melodramatic overclaims ("restructured the conditions under which," "transformed how we")
- [ ] Contributions are stated directly — not via gap-bridging ("this paper bridges the gap between")

---

## R4 · Academic Citation Logic

- [ ] Sources are used for the specific claim they establish — not pulled in to sound supported
- [ ] No contrastive citation framing that manufactures a contradiction between studies in different domains
- [ ] Citation chains don't rely on secondary sources for primary theoretical claims
- [ ] Peer-reviewed sources for key claims; track edition/URL/access date for web sources

---

## R5 · Concreteness & Mechanism

- [ ] Abstract category labels (infrastructure, the system, the platform) replaced with named mechanisms (the ranking algorithm, the data retention pipeline)
- [ ] Specific instances used: named people, named studies, named datasets, named platforms — not category labels
- [ ] Consequences named in the subject's own terms, not the analyst's framing
- [ ] Data embedded in prose clauses, not appended in parentheses or bolted to the end of a sentence

---

## R6 · Voice & Register (Academic)

- [ ] Register is consistent within each section (DH/DHQ: precise, accessible, peer-reviewed)
- [ ] Abstract terms match the actual subject position — check that the framing fits the agent being described
- [ ] Passive voice used intentionally (to foreground institutional agents) not as default
- [ ] First-person "I" used where the author's design decisions are the subject — not hidden behind passive constructions

---

## R7 · Humanizer Pass

- [ ] At least one short sentence following a long sentence in each dense paragraph
- [ ] Specific named instances used at least once per section instead of category labels
- [ ] Sentence openers vary across the section — no three consecutive "The" openers
- [ ] One unexpected but precise word per paragraph (not jargon — the exact right word)
- [ ] Trust transitions — cut "In this section, I will argue that..." and start arguing
