# CHECKLIST_COPY.md
## Sentence-level copy edit pass
*Use this file on a completed draft before submitting or sharing. Scan each section top to bottom.*

---

## C1 · Punctuation & Connectors

- [ ] No em dashes or en dashes used as punctuation
- [ ] No colon where a subordinate clause or connector works (requiring, so that, since, with, where...while)
- [ ] No semicolon unless the two clauses have no expressible logical connector, or it's a deliberate rhetorical pause
- [ ] Colons used only for colon-restatement (general claim [colon] named specifics) or display (before code/blockquote)

---

## C2 · Banned Vocabulary

- [ ] No AI slop: delve, tapestry, vibrant, crucial, comprehensive, meticulous, embark, robust, seamless, groundbreaking, synergy, transformative, paramount, multifaceted, myriad, cornerstone, reimagine, empower, catalyst, invaluable, bustling, nestled, realm
- [ ] No business jargon verbs: utilize, leverage, facilitate, implement, enhance, optimize, foster, drive
- [ ] No invented academic noun phrases not used in the actual literature
- [ ] No vague intensifiers: "with particular force," "especially acutely," "with renewed urgency"

---

## C3 · Banned Constructions

- [ ] No em-dash contrastive pivots: "not X but Y," "not just X," "not only X but also Y," "less about X more about Y"
- [ ] No setup phrases: "What makes this interesting is...," "Here's the thing:," "The key insight is..."
- [ ] No gap-bridging: "bridges the gap," "fills the gap between," "the gap between X and Y"
- [ ] No "What X does as Y applies/shows/reveals..." opener (nominalizing "What" construction)
- [ ] No trailing significance participles: "highlighting its importance," "reflecting broader trends," "underscoring its role as"
- [ ] No bridging constructions: "is what," "is this:," "What X is Y," "is what happens"

---

## C4 · Sentence Structure

- [ ] No interrupted subjects: "Subject, [qualifier], verb" — move qualifier after verb or rewrite
- [ ] No abstract concepts as subjects of short sentences ("Meaning played no part." "The distribution was...")
- [ ] No list subjects, especially two consecutive list-subject sentences
- [ ] No nominalization chains: "[gerund]'s [noun] of [noun]" before the verb
- [ ] Subjects are short and concrete — people, things, actions
- [ ] No "in order to" (use "to"); no "due to the fact that" (use "because")

---

## C5 · Repetition & Rhythm

- [ ] No anaphora abuse — same sentence opener used 3+ times in succession
- [ ] No tricolon abuse — more than one tricolon per argument cluster
- [ ] Sentence variation: mix short and long; vary openers; at least one short sentence after a long one

---

## C6 · Citation & Attribution

- [ ] Researchers are grammatical subjects of sentences describing their studies ("Devlin et al. found..." not "The finding that...")
- [ ] No false dilemma framing between studies operating in different domains (submarine rule)
- [ ] No blog posts or aggregator sites doing load-bearing theoretical work
- [ ] Every non-trivial claim tied to a traceable citation
