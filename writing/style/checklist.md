# Revision Checklist

*Run this on every draft before it leaves the workspace.*  
*Load alongside the relevant scale file(s) for targeted edits.*

---

## Voice and Register

- [ ] Does this sound like a person thinking, or a model generating?
- [ ] Is the register right for the venue (DHQ, Journalism Studies, essay)?
- [ ] Is the tone too smooth? Too polished? (If yes, loosen one construction per paragraph)
- [ ] Are claims bounded to what the evidence shows?

## Structure

- [ ] Stakes clear by paragraph one?
- [ ] Does the opening start with the thing itself, or with background/context?
- [ ] Does the ending extend or reframe — not summarize?
- [ ] Is there one clear, statable claim the piece makes?
- [ ] Does each paragraph do one thing?

## Paragraph Level

- [ ] Is evidence embedded in prose (not footnoted or parenthetical where it could be integrated)?
- [ ] Is there at least one serious counterargument addressed per major claim cluster?
- [ ] Are transitions doing work, or announcing work?

## Sentence Level — Banned Constructions

- [ ] No contrastive pivots ("not X but Y," "not just X," "more than X")
- [ ] No interrupted subjects ("Subject, [qualifier], verb")
- [ ] No abstract subjects in short stub sentences
- [ ] No bridging constructions ("is what," "is this:," "What X is Y")
- [ ] No "What X does" openers
- [ ] No "in this light" mid-sentence
- [ ] No list subjects (two or more consecutive list-subject sentences = hard stop)
- [ ] No trailing significance participles ("-ing" clause that can be removed without information loss)
- [ ] No anaphora repetition (same opener 3+ times in succession)
- [ ] No nominalizations ("[gerund]'s [noun] of [noun]" before the verb)

## Diction

- [ ] No AI slop vocabulary (delve, tapestry, vibrant, crucial, etc.)
- [ ] No business jargon verbs (utilize, leverage, facilitate, enhance, optimize)
- [ ] No vague intensifiers ("with particular force," "especially acutely")
- [ ] No invented academic noun phrases
- [ ] No negative definitions (define by what IS present, not by absence)
- [ ] No filler ("in order to" → "to"; "due to the fact that" → "because")

## Verbs

- [ ] Verb audit done? Is each verb the most precise option available?
- [ ] No weak copula constructions that could carry more meaning ("is shaped by" → find the verb)
- [ ] Logical connectors used instead of conjunctive ones where applicable?

## Colons and Semicolons

- [ ] Every colon and semicolon audited for hidden logical connector?
- [ ] Colons used only for legitimate restatement or display purposes?

## Citations and Claims

- [ ] No blog posts or aggregators doing theoretical load-bearing work
- [ ] Authors doing the research (not findings): "Veldre et al. found X" not "the finding that X shows Y"
- [ ] No false dilemmas in contrastive citation
- [ ] Data embedded at the point of consequence, not as afterthought
- [ ] Brackets [X] used for unconfirmed data; no vague hedges ("many," "significant numbers")

## Final Read

- [ ] Read one paragraph aloud. Does it breathe? If you'd pause for breath mid-sentence, break it.
- [ ] One surprising, precise word per paragraph?
- [ ] Short sentence placed after the longest sentence in each section?
