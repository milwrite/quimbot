## Governing Principles

These apply at every scale. Load them regardless of task.

**1. Researchers do the work, not findings.**  
Put the author at the grammatical subject. "Veldre et al. found X" not "the finding that X shows Y."

**2. Concrete before abstract.**  
Give the example, event, or name first. Inference follows.

**3. Name what acts.**  
Not "the platform" — the ranking algorithm, the data retention pipeline, the administrative subpoena. If you can't name it, the claim isn't ready.

**4. No gap-bridging.**  
State what the piece does. Don't describe the distance it crosses.

**5. Claims need traceable evidence.**  
No floating assertions. No blog posts doing load-bearing theoretical work.

**6. Bound the claim to the evidence.**  
"The compliance record reveals..." not "this shows that all platforms..."
