# Diction — Word-Level Rules

*Load this for: word choice, phrase trimming, terminology decisions*

---

## Banned Words (AI slop)

Never use: delve, tapestry, vibrant, crucial, comprehensive, meticulous, embark, robust, seamless, groundbreaking, synergy, transformative, paramount, multifaceted, myriad, cornerstone, reimagine, empower, catalyst, invaluable, bustling, nestled, realm.

## Banned Verbs (business jargon)

Never use: utilize, leverage, facilitate, implement, enhance, optimize, foster, drive (business sense).

Use instead: build, cut, break, run, ship, fix, test, push, pull, grab, drop, hit, show, reveal, compel, generate, push.

## Verb Strength Audit

On every revision pass: for each sentence, ask whether the verb is the most descriptively precise option available.

Weak copula constructions ("are X-mediated", "is shaped by") almost always have stronger replacements.

**Before:** "participation trajectories are platform-mediated outcomes"  
**After:** "participation trajectories encode the pressures of the infrastructure they run through"

Outsourcing meaning to an adjective is a signal to stop and find the verb.

## Vague Intensifiers — Ban

Kill: "with particular force," "especially acutely," "with renewed urgency," and any similar phrase that amplifies without adding content.

If something applies particularly, name precisely why. If you can't, cut the intensifier.

## No Invented Academic Noun Phrases

LLMs mint noun phrases that sound scholarly but appear nowhere in actual literature.  
Examples to refuse: "LLM-derived predictability," "semantic retrieval interference," "distributional alignment pressure," "cloze-norming divergence."

**Test:** Would a researcher in the relevant field use this phrase? If not, don't coin it.  
**Fix:** Describe the thing with a verb. "LLMs generate better regression predictors for reading-time data than offline cloze norms do."

## Negative Definitions — Ban

Don't define by absence. "Patterns that no reader encounters as such" defines by who doesn't see it.

**Test:** If the characterization is entirely negative (no X, unavailable to Y, never Z), rewrite to say what IS present, what IS available, what DOES happen.

**Before:** "available only as weighted co-occurrence patterns that no reader encounters as such"  
**After:** "remediated as weighted co-occurrence patterns"

## Filler — Cut

"In order to" → "to"  
"Due to the fact that" → "because"  
"The fact that X" → restructure

## One Surprising Word Per Paragraph

Find the generic noun and replace it once per paragraph with the exact right word. Not jargon — the word that signals a thinking writer looked at this.
