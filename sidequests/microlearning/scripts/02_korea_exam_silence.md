**Hook (5s):**

**Key Insight (25s):**
Every November, South Korea takes the Suneung—the college entrance exam that determines your entire future. On that day, the whole nation reorganizes around 500,000 students. Planes are grounded during listening sections. Stock markets open late. Police escort late students with sirens blaring. Even military drills stop. It's not just a test—it's a national event that reveals how much pressure a society can put on a single moment.
