# Sentence-Level Rules

*Line-by-line habits. Dense ruleset. Read during edit passes.*

---

## Core Sentence Habits

**Simple subjects.** Lead with people, things, or actions. No noun-phrase pileups before the verb.

**Sentence variation.** Mix short and long. Vary openers. Use fragments for emphasis. Don't start three sentences in a row with "The." Mix: time markers, verbs, prepositional phrases, names.

**Concrete nouns.** Prefer named things over abstract categories. The rule: if you can point to it, name it.

**One short sentence after a long one.** After a long, clause-heavy sentence, drop a short one. It lands harder and reads like thought, not output.

---

## Hard Banned Constructions

### Em dashes
Never. Use periods, commas, semicolons, parentheses, or colons (for lists/definitions only). When in doubt, restructure.

### Contrastive pivots
Kill: "not X but Y," "not just X," "more than X," "beyond that," "not merely X but Y," "not only X but also Y," "less about X more about Y."
State the point directly.

### Interrupted subjects
**Pattern to ban:** "Subject, [qualifier], verb."
- Bad: "The second debate, equally consequential for what the procedure actually measures, concerned deletion method."
- Bad: "Taylor, working within the Gestalt tradition, developed the cloze procedure."
Fix: Move qualifier after the verb, make it a separate sentence, or rewrite as a subordinate clause.
- After: "The second debate concerned deletion method and carried equal weight for what the procedure actually measures."
Exception: Very short appositives of two or three words ("Oller, a linguist, argued...") are acceptable.

### Bridging constructions
Kill: "is what," "is this:," "What X is Y," "is what happens when."
Test: If you can delete "is what" or "is this:" and the sentence parses, delete it.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| The noise is what makes it feel drawn | The noise makes it feel drawn |
| The difficulty is this: near the boundary... | The difficulty: a third basin appears near the boundary... |
| Flocking is what happens when every bird... | Flocking happens when every bird... |

### Nominalization ban
No verb-phrase nominalizations. Pattern: `[gerund/noun]'s [noun] of [noun]` before the verb = always rewrite as two sentences.
- "pretraining's reduction of text to token distributions" → "pretraining reduces text to token distributions"

### "What X does" openers
Kill: "What Gitelman and Jackson describe as X applies to..." → "Gitelman and Jackson describe X as it applies to..."
Kill: "What this shows is..." → "This shows..."

### Abstract subjects in short sentences
Short sentences where an abstraction is the grammatical subject doing nothing: "Meaning played no part. The distribution was the object."
Fix: Fold the claim into the person, method, or action it belongs to. "Harris set meaning aside entirely, arguing that..."
Test: Short sentence + abstract subject + weak copula verb (is/was/played/remained) = restructure.

### Colon and semicolon overuse
Colons and semicolons in body prose are usually hiding a logical connector. Surface it.

| Relationship | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Elaboration | participial phrase: "requiring that," "consisting of" |
| Result | "so that" |
| Parallel elaboration | "with," "where...while" |
| Causal | "since," "because" |

The one legitimate colon: general claim [colon] named specifics. "Educational cloze tests assess X: the synthesis of Y, Z, and W."
Display colons (before code blocks, block quotes) are exempt.
Semicolons: same audit. Exception: rhetorical punch in a short two-part sentence ("The player is not simulating the inference engine; they are one").

### Tricolon abuse
A single rule-of-three is elegant. Multiple tricolons back-to-back is a pattern-recognition failure. Keep one per argument cluster. Break the others.

### Anaphora abuse
Don't repeat the same sentence opener multiple times in quick succession.
- Bad: "They assume that... They assume that... They assume that..."
Exception: Deliberate rhetorical anaphora, once per piece, clearly intentional.

### Trailing participles
Ban tacking significance onto the end of a sentence with a present participle.
Banned: "highlighting its importance," "reflecting broader trends," "contributing to the development of...," "underscoring its role as...," "showcasing how...," "demonstrating the enduring legacy of..."
Fix: Delete the participial phrase entirely, or state the actual claim as its own sentence.
Test: If the -ing clause can be removed without losing information, remove it.

### Negative definitions
Don't define a thing by what it isn't or who doesn't see it.
- Before: "available only as weighted co-occurrence patterns that no reader encounters as such"
- After: "remediated as weighted co-occurrence patterns"
Test: If your characterization is entirely negative (no X, unavailable to Y, never Z), rewrite to say what IS present.

### Logical prepositions over conjunctive ones
When two elements are related by a preposition, prefer a logical connector (cause, consequence, contrast, purpose) over a conjunctive one (addition, sequence).
Test: Can "and," "while," "as well as" be expressed as "to," "so that," "because," "which reveals"? Make the logical relationship explicit.
- Before: "I trace these genealogies and interrogate the divergences..."
- After: "I trace these genealogies to show where the divergences lie..."
Not a rule against "and": short parallel constructions ("build, test, push") are fine.

### Gap-bridging constructions
Ban: "the gap between X and Y," "bridges the gap," "this paper bridges," "fills the gap between."
State what the paper does. Don't describe the distance it crosses.

### List subjects
Never construct the subject of a sentence as a list, especially following another list. Find the singular subject. Two consecutive list-subject sentences is a hard stop.

### "In this light"
Never: "X, in this light, Y." It stalls the sentence and signals hedging. If the logical connection is real, the verb makes it.

### Filler phrases
"in order to" → "to"
"due to the fact that" → "because"
If a phrase adds no meaning, delete it.
