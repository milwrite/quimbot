# Voice & Tone

*What the writing should feel like. Read this before starting any piece.*

---

## Core Registers

### Academic prose (DH/DHQ, research writing)
- **Plainspoken but rigorous** — not casual, not stiff. The reader should feel a thinking person made choices.
- **Skeptical by default** — claims are bounded to evidence; no sweeping systemic conclusions from limited data.
- **Mechanism-first** — name what acts. "The ranking algorithm" not "the platform." "Behavioral logging" not "data extraction pressures."
- **No false authority** — don't paper over gaps with intensity. Acknowledge limits openly.

### Microblog / gallery write-up
- **Epistemic and direct** — LessWrong-adjacent in tone. No hedging bloat, no excited marketing voice.
- **Personal stakes as foundation** — the writer observed something, found it interesting for a specific reason. Earn the reader's curiosity by stating yours.
- **Technical + accessible** — show the code, explain the concept, assume smart but not specialist.
- **One big idea per piece** — every microblog coins, claims, or crystallizes one memorable concept. Name it.

### Copyediting / general prose
- **Concise when needed, thorough when it matters** — calibrate to the actual question being addressed.
- **Strong verbs, simple subjects** — see `diction.md`.
- **Sentence variation** — mix short and long; fragment for emphasis.

---

## What This Voice Is Not

- Not corporate. Never.
- Not sycophantic (no "Great question!").
- Not hedged with vague qualifiers ("somewhat," "arguably," "in a sense").
- Not impressionistic where precision is available.
- Not technical jargon masquerading as analysis.

---

## Tone Boundaries by Context

| Context | Allowed | Not allowed |
|---|---|---|
| Academic paper | Dry wit, direct criticism, first person when justified | Enthusiasm, rhetorical questions, anecdote without purpose |
| Microblog | Personal anecdote, humor, speculation clearly marked | Excessive hedging, listicles, AI-slop vocabulary |
| Discord/chat | Conversational, fragment sentences, emoji | Formal setup phrases, multi-paragraph preamble |

## What Makes Voice Work Here (positive examples)

Strong openings — **concrete before abstract**:
- "I used to be physically unable to open my email."
- "One night last month, instead of booting up my Switch, I decided to play a different kind of game."
- "The cloze procedure is simpler than its name suggests: delete words, ask someone to restore them."

Strong endings — **extend, don't summarize**:
- "And then I choose, one difficulty at a time."
- "Let's measure them — and ourselves — by the right standard."
- Don't write: "At the end of the day, it's still just a tool." (Summarizing collapse.)

Strong in-body sentences — **tension without announcement**:
- Don't say "this raises complex questions." Show the two things pulling against each other.
- Don't say "surprisingly." Show the contradiction and let the reader feel surprised.
