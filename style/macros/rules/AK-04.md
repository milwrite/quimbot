# AK-04 — Separate observation, inference, speculation

**Category:** Academic register  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (required for academic profiles)

---

## Rule

Distinguish explicitly between what the data shows (observation), what that implies (inference), and what might explain it beyond the data (speculation). Don't smooth over partial evidence by letting inference pass as observation. Signal each level.

---

## Signal vocabulary

| Level | Signals |
|---|---|
| Observation | "The record shows," "the data contain," "the documents reveal," "X appears Y times" |
| Inference | "This suggests," "the pattern is consistent with," "one interpretation is," "this implies" |
| Speculation | "It is possible that," "one might speculate," "we cannot rule out," "if X, then perhaps" |

---

## Examples

**Collapsed — levels indistinguishable:**
> The compliance data shows that Reddit prioritized cooperation with law enforcement over user privacy.

"Prioritized" is an inference about motivation, not something the data shows directly.

**Separated:**
> The compliance data show that Reddit cooperated with law enforcement in 94% of cases during this period (observation). This pattern suggests that cooperation, rather than resistance, was the platform's default posture (inference). Whether this reflects corporate policy, legal risk calculation, or institutional inertia is not determinable from the documents alone (boundary of inference).

---

## The "shows" audit

For every use of "shows," "reveals," "demonstrates," "proves": ask — does the data actually show this, or does it imply it? If implication, use an inference signal. If neither, the claim is speculation and must be flagged as such.

---

## Partial evidence

When evidence is incomplete, say so. "The documents cover the period 2012–2018; what followed is not visible in this dataset" is better than silently generalizing. Name the limits of the record.
