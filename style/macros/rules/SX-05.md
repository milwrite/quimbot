# SX-05 — What-X opener

**Category:** Syntax  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Kill any sentence opening with "What X shows/reveals/describes/demonstrates/does is Y." These constructions defer the real subject by turning the predicate into the grammatical subject. Find the agent and lead with it.

---

## Diagnostic

Does the sentence begin with "What"? Stop. Find who or what actually does the action and start there.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> What Taylor's procedure shows is that deletion method determines difficulty more than gap position.

> What the cloze task actually measures is not lexical prediction but contextual recovery.

> What makes the compliance record significant is the timeline of cooperation.

**Good:**
> Taylor's procedure shows that deletion method determines difficulty more than gap position.

> The cloze task measures contextual recovery, not lexical prediction.

> The compliance record's significance lies in the timeline of cooperation.

---

## Fixes

1. Delete "What ... is" and rewrite so the actual subject leads
2. If the sentence is trying to emphasize a contrast, use a short preceding sentence to set up the contrast, then state it directly
