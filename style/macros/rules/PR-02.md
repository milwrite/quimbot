# PR-02 — Opener variation

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Vary sentence openers. Three or more sentences in a row opening with the same syntactic structure (subject-verb, "The [noun]", "This [noun]") reads as a list. Mix: lead with a subordinate clause, a participial phrase, an adverb, a prepositional phrase, or a short fragment.

---

## Diagnostic

Read five consecutive sentences and note how each opens. Are two or more starting with "The [same noun type]"? Are three starting with a bare subject-verb? Restructure at least one.

---

## Examples

**Monotonous — all subject-verb:**
> The compliance record spans five years. The FOIA documents reveal consistent patterns. The platform cooperated in all but three cases. The exceptions cluster in 2015–2016.

**Varied:**
> Over five years, Reddit's compliance record reveals consistent patterns. In all but three cases, the platform cooperated — the exceptions cluster in 2015–2016, during the policy transition period. What the documents show, taken together, is a pattern of voluntary cooperation that precedes legal compulsion.

---

## Opener inventory

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Subordinate clause | "Although the platform resisted in 2015..." |
| Participial phrase | "Working within established guidelines..." |
| Prepositional phrase | "In all but three cases..." |
| Time/place adverb | "Between 2012 and 2018..." |
| Short fragment | "Three exceptions." |
| Inverted structure | "More surprising is the timeline." |

---

## Constraint

Don't manufacture variety that distorts meaning. Opener variation is a service to rhythm; it should never put a modifier in a misleading position. "Working within established guidelines, the researchers found..." only works if the researchers were the ones working within the guidelines.
