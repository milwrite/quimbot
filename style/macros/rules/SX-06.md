# SX-06 — Logical prepositions over conjunctive ones

**Category:** Syntax  
**Added:** 2026-04-06 (from STYLE_GUIDE.md 2026-03-21)  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

When a preposition or conjunction relates two elements, prefer a logical connector — showing cause, consequence, contrast, or purpose — over a conjunctive one that shows only addition or temporal sequence.

---

## Test

Can the relationship made by "and," "while," or "as well as" be expressed as a logical relationship? "to," "so that," "because," "which reveals," "since"? If yes, use the logical connector.

---

## Examples

**Weak — conjunctive:**
> I trace these genealogies and interrogate the divergences...

> I kept each task separate, with no information carried forward.

> The compliance record spans five years and shows consistent cooperation.

**Strong — logical:**
> I trace these genealogies to show where the divergences lie...

> I kept each task separate so that no information carries forward.

> The compliance record spans five years, documenting consistent cooperation across a period that predates formal compulsion.

---

## What this is NOT

A rule against "and." Short parallel constructions ("build, test, push") are fine. Coordinate subjects and compound predicates at equal logical weight are fine.

The rule targets compound predicates where the second element is the logical consequence or purpose of the first. When the second verb can answer "why?" or "to what end?" about the first, use a logical connector.

---

## Upgrade inventory

| Conjunctive | Possible logical connector |
|---|---|
| and [consequence] | so that, which caused, producing |
| and [purpose] | to, in order to (then cut "in order") |
| while [contrast] | whereas, although, despite |
| as [temporal] | when, after, before |
| with [result] | such that, yielding, revealing |
