# DC-09 — No melodramatic overclaims

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (required for academic profiles)

---

## Rule

No sweeping systemic claims from single events. No "restructured the conditions under which," no "transformed how we understand," no claims that exceed what the evidence shows. Bound claims to what the evidence demonstrates; name the specific change, not the category it belongs to.

---

## Patterns to ban

- "...transformed the way X..."
- "...fundamentally changed how..."
- "...restructured the conditions under which..."
- "...revolutionized our understanding of..."
- "...represents a paradigm shift in..."
- "...challenged everything we thought we knew about..."

---

## Why

Overclaims undermine credibility. A reader who encounters "this study transformed how we understand reading" will skeptically question what evidence could possibly support that claim. If the evidence can't support the claim, the claim must be cut.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> The publication of Taylor's 1953 paper transformed how linguists understood reading comprehension.

> Reddit's 2015 policy change restructured the conditions under which student participation in campus communities became possible.

**Good:**
> Taylor's 1953 paper introduced the cloze procedure, which became a standard measure in reading research by the late 1960s.

> Reddit's 2015 content policy eliminated several large communities and made content moderation rules visible to users who had previously treated them as natural features of the interface.

---

## The principle

Name the specific change. Name the specific evidence for it. Let the reader draw the conclusion about significance. Don't announce significance; show it.

---
