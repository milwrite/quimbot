# PR-04 — Trailing -ing (participial tag-on)

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (all profiles)

---

## Rule

No participial phrase tacked onto the end of a sentence as a trailing addition. These tag-ons ("...suggesting that," "...making it," "...allowing users to") dilute the sentence's force and obscure the logical relationship between the main clause and the addition. Restructure: make the participial a separate sentence or convert it to a subordinate clause with an explicit conjunction.

---

## Diagnostic

Does the sentence end with a comma + present participle (-ing phrase)? That's the pattern to eliminate.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> The ranking algorithm sorts posts by recency, burying contributions that arrive after the early engagement window.

> Reddit cooperated with law enforcement before SESTA/FOSTA, making voluntary compliance the norm rather than the exception.

> The cloze procedure generates completion probabilities, allowing researchers to estimate predictability at each word position.

**Good:**
> The ranking algorithm sorts posts by recency. Contributions that arrive after the early engagement window are buried.

> Reddit cooperated with law enforcement before SESTA/FOSTA required it; voluntary compliance was already the norm.

> The cloze procedure generates completion probabilities, which researchers use to estimate predictability at each word position. [relative clause is fine here]

---

## Exception

Participial phrases that genuinely modify the subject (not tag on to the predicate) are acceptable when placed before or immediately after the subject:

> **Acceptable:** "Working within the Gestalt tradition, Taylor developed the cloze procedure." [modifies Taylor; positioned correctly]

> **Banned:** "Taylor developed the cloze procedure, working within the Gestalt tradition." [tag-on at end]

---

## Why this matters

A trailing -ing phrase makes the logical relationship vague. "...making X" could mean "so that X," "which caused X," "with the result that X," or "thereby X" — the sentence refuses to commit. Forcing the restructure forces the writer to name the actual relationship.
