# DC-10 — Vague intensifiers

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06 (from STYLE_GUIDE.md 2026-03-18)  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Ban vague intensifier phrases that amplify without adding content. If something applies particularly, name precisely why or to what degree. If you can't, cut the intensifier and let the claim stand on its own.

---

## Banned phrases

- "with particular force"
- "especially acutely"
- "with renewed urgency"
- "with special intensity"
- "with striking clarity"
- "in especially significant ways"
- "particularly clearly"
- "notably"
- "strikingly"

---

## Why

These phrases announce that something is especially true without saying what makes it especially true. They substitute gesture for precision. The reader cannot verify the claim because the intensifier contains no content.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> This applies with particular force to platform governance.

> The compliance record speaks especially acutely to the question of voluntary cooperation.

**Good:**
> Platform governance depends on voluntary compliance rather than enforcement — which is why the compliance record is the primary evidence.

> The compliance record documents cooperation that predates legal compulsion, which makes voluntary cooperation the operative explanation.

---

## Fix

Delete the intensifier. If the sentence collapses without it, the sentence doesn't have a claim. Find the claim and state it directly.

---
