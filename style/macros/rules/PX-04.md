# PX-04 — Semicolon discipline

**Category:** Punctuation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

A semicolon joins two independent clauses that are so closely related the period would feel abrupt. Do not use it as a comma substitute between items in a list unless the list items themselves contain commas. Do not use more than two semicolons in a paragraph without checking whether a period is better.

---

## Diagnostic

Can you replace the semicolon with a period and have two complete, sensible sentences? If yes, the semicolon is optional — check whether the connection is tight enough to justify it. If the two clauses could stand alone without loss, a period is almost always cleaner.

---

## Examples

**Good — tight logical connection:**
> The model processes a sequence; the reader recovers a word.

The contrast between the two operations is the point. The semicolon holds the comparison in one sentence.

**Bad — period would be cleaner:**
> The compliance record spans five years; the FOIA request returned 847 documents.

These are two facts. A period is cleaner than a semicolon here because there's no tight logical hinge between them.

**Good — list with internal commas:**
> Three factors shaped participation: the ranking algorithm, which sorts by recency; the moderation system, which flags by volume; and the data pipeline, which logs interactions.

---

## Fixes

When in doubt: use the period. The semicolon earns its place only when separation would make the connection feel abrupt or when list items contain internal commas.
