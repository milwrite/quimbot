# DC-05 — "This" requires a noun

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (all profiles)

---

## Rule

"This" standing alone as a subject is banned. Always follow "This" with the noun it names. "This constraint," "This result," "This approach" — always name what you're pointing at.

---

## Why

Bare "This" as subject signals the writer hasn't named what they're pointing at. The reader must look back and infer. Naming the referent keeps the argument explicit and prevents the reader from attaching "this" to the wrong antecedent.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> The cloze procedure generates completion probabilities at each gap. This allows researchers to estimate predictability.

> Reddit cooperated with law enforcement before SESTA/FOSTA. This is significant.

**Good:**
> The cloze procedure generates completion probabilities at each gap. These probabilities allow researchers to estimate predictability at each word position.

> Reddit cooperated with law enforcement before SESTA/FOSTA. This timeline matters: it predates the platform's public commitment to user privacy.

---

## The "these" corollary

When a noun has already been introduced, refer back to it with "these" rather than "the" to keep the connection explicit. "These forums" not "the forums" after forums have been introduced. "The" can float free of its referent; "these" anchors.

---

## Revision pass

Grep for " This " (standalone "This" as subject). Every instance must be fixed before output ships.
