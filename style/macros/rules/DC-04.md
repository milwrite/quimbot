# DC-04 — No setup phrases

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (strong revision habit)

---

## Rule

Never write a sentence that announces a point instead of making it. Cut the announcement. Make the point.

---

## Banned setup phrases

- "What makes this interesting is..."
- "What's remarkable is..."
- "Here's the thing:"
- "The key insight is..."
- "What's important here is..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "The point I'm making is..."
- "What this tells us is..."
- "The takeaway here is..."
- "In essence,"
- "Simply put,"
- "To put it another way,"

---

## Why

Setup phrases delay the argument. They also signal that the writer isn't confident the point will land on its own. If the point needs a setup, the problem is usually in the point, not in the reader. Fix the point.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> What's remarkable about the compliance record is that Reddit cooperated before the law required it.

> The key insight here is that deletion and erasure are not the same thing.

**Good:**
> The compliance record shows cooperation that precedes legal compulsion.

> Deletion and erasure are not the same thing.

---

## Partial exception

"To summarize:" and "In brief:" at the start of a section recap are acceptable when the recap follows a long argument. These are structural signals, not setup phrases. Even here, prefer a topic sentence that restates the argument directly over an explicit recap label.
