# DC-11 — No negative definitions

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06 (from STYLE_GUIDE.md 2026-03-18)  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Don't define a thing by what it isn't, who doesn't see it, or what it lacks. Name where the thing goes, what it does, what IS present.

---

## Diagnostic

Does the characterization consist entirely of negatives? "No reader encounters," "invisible to users," "never directly observable," "no one can see" — these tell the reader what isn't happening, which is not a description. Find the positive form.

---

## Examples

**Bad — negative definitions:**
> ...available only as weighted co-occurrence patterns that no reader encounters as such.

> ...a form that remains invisible to ordinary users.

> ...something no one can observe directly.

**Good — positive form:**
> ...remediated as weighted co-occurrence patterns.

> ...a form users encounter only through its effects — slower load times, changed recommendation queues, altered visibility.

> ...detectable only through the behavioral outcomes the system produces.

---

## The test

If your characterization is entirely negative ("no X," "unavailable to Y," "never Z"), rewrite to say what IS present, what IS available, what DOES happen.

---

## Legitimate negative constructions

"The model has no long-range interaction" is acceptable — it characterizes the model's architecture by the absence of a specific feature, which is precisely the relevant information. The ban targets characterizations where the thing is defined only by what it isn't, with no positive description offered at all.

The test: does the negative characterization tell the reader something specific and useful? Or does it substitute absence for description?
