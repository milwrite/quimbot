# CT-02 — Same-question test for contrastive citation

**Category:** Citation  
**Added:** 2026-03-29  
**Non-negotiable:** No (but required in academic-draft, cloze-paper, reddit-piece profiles)

---

## Rule

Before writing any sentence that contrasts two sources, write out explicitly:

1. What precise question does each study answer?
2. What is the dependent variable in each?
3. Are the populations and measures comparable?

If the answers differ on any axis — describe each source in its own domain. Do not manufacture a contest.

---

## Diagnostic

**If you cannot write down both questions clearly, you don't understand the contrast yet. Do not write the contrastive sentence.**

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> While Jacobs et al. found divergence, Veldre et al. found alignment — contradicting the divergence claim.

Manufactured contest. The two studies answer different questions with different dependent variables.

**Good:**
> Jacobs et al. measured whether LLM token predictions align with human cloze-completion responses. Veldre et al. measured whether LLM-derived predictability fits eye-movement regression data better than human cloze norms. These are different dependent variables in different experimental paradigms.

Each study gets its own domain. No contest is manufactured.

---

## Relation to CT-01

CT-01 (submarine rule) states the principle. CT-02 makes it operational with a concrete pre-writing procedure.

---

## Procedure (before drafting any contrastive sentence)

```
Study A: [question] / [DV] / [population] / [measure]
Study B: [question] / [DV] / [population] / [measure]

Same question? Y/N
Same DV?       Y/N
Comparable?    Y/N

If any N → describe separately. Do not contrast.
```
