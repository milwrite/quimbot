# PR-06 — No freestanding echo sentences

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

No short sentences that restate or lightly extend what the previous sentence already said. Stub sentences that echo the preceding clause ("The forums work because they persist." following a sentence that just made that point) land as false emphasis. Fold the point into the sentence doing the actual work, or cut it.

---

## Diagnostic

After writing a short sentence, read the sentence before it. Does the short sentence add new information, contrast, or surprise? Or does it just say the same thing again slightly differently? If the latter: fold or cut.

---

## Examples

**Bad — echo:**
> Participation trajectories encode the pressures of the infrastructure they run through. Infrastructure shapes participation.

> The compliance record spans five years and shows consistent cooperation. The pattern is consistent.

> Taylor developed the cloze procedure in 1953. The procedure dates to 1953.

**Good — short sentence that earns its place through contrast or new information:**
> Participation trajectories encode the pressures of the infrastructure they run through. None of this is visible to the user.

> The compliance record spans five years and shows consistent cooperation. Three exceptions cluster in 2015–2016.

---

## The test

A short sentence earns its place through:
1. **Contrast** with the preceding sentence
2. **New information** not present in the preceding sentence
3. **Surprise** — the short sentence says something the reader didn't expect

It does not earn its place by:
- Repeating the same point in fewer words
- Naming what the previous sentence already demonstrated
- Announcing what the paragraph just showed
