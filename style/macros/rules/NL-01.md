# NL-01 — DHQ register and structural continuity

**Category:** NLG / generation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (cloze paper profile)

---

## Rule

All prose generated for the Cloze Reader paper must match DHQ's register: peer-reviewed humanities journal with a digital methods emphasis. Structural continuity must be maintained across sessions — each generated section must connect explicitly to the sections before and after it. The functional asymmetry claim governs the paper's central argument and must be consistently applied.

---

## DHQ register characteristics

- Academic prose, not technical documentation
- Evidence-led argument, not conceptual survey
- First-person plural ("we") for joint authorship; hedged claims for contested terrain
- Sources cited by author-date in text; full bibliography required
- No bullet points or headers in prose sections — argument advances through paragraphs
- Section breaks at structural transitions, not at every topic shift

---

## The functional asymmetry claim

The paper's central empirical claim: cloze completion and language model prediction are not two versions of the same operation. A reader filling in a blank recovers a word within a constructed context; a language model assigns probability to a next token given a training distribution. These processes diverge in ways that make cloze-derived norms and LLM surprisal measures non-equivalent — even when they produce similar numerical outputs.

**Every generated section must be consistent with this claim.** Any prose that implies the two operations are equivalent, or that frames them as "better vs. worse versions" of the same thing, violates NL-01.

---

## Structural continuity protocol

Before generating any new section or revision:
1. Read the immediately preceding section in `writing/cloze-reader-paper/draft.md`
2. Identify the last argumentative move made
3. Generate text that explicitly connects to or advances from that move
4. Flag any transition that is unclear or requires a bridging sentence

---

## Register violations to catch

- Technical vocabulary without explanation (DHQ readers know digital methods; they may not know psycholinguistics details)
- Missing hedges on contested empirical claims
- Bullet-point summaries in argument sections
- Headers inside a continuous argument section
- First-person singular ("I") without coordinating with Petrarch
