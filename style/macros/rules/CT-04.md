# CT-04 — Citation sequence

**Category:** Citation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (required for academic profiles)

---

## Rule

Introduce a scholar after the phenomenon. Show the thing first; let the theoretical term arrive as an explanation of something the reader has already felt. "The forums work because they persist. Star and Ruhleder would call this infrastructure" does the work. "Star and Ruhleder describe infrastructure as X, and these forums fit that description" runs through a checklist without making an argument.

---

## Why

When the scholar arrives before the phenomenon, the reader is asked to hold a theoretical definition in mind while waiting for the example that fills it. This reverses the order of understanding. The example earns the theory; the theory explains the example. The sequence encodes which one is doing the work.

---

## Examples

**Bad — scholar before phenomenon:**
> Star and Ruhleder (1996) describe infrastructure as that which becomes visible upon breakdown. Reddit's moderation policies illustrate this pattern.

> According to Hall (1980), encoding/decoding describes how audiences interpret texts through their own social frames. Reddit comment threads demonstrate this process.

**Good — phenomenon before scholar:**
> In 2015, Reddit's decision to ban r/fatpeoplehate surfaced the platform's content governance for millions of users who had previously treated community rules as natural features of the interface. Star and Ruhleder would call this infrastructure — systems that become visible upon breakdown.

> Users reading a post about campus housing interpret it through their own experience of housing insecurity, financial pressure, or institutional distrust — not through the post's intended framing. Hall (1980) names this negotiated decoding.

---

## The structure

1. Describe the phenomenon in the subject's own terms or through observation
2. Name the scholar and term as an explanation of what the reader just saw
3. Let the citation confirm the argument rather than introduce it

---

## Exception

Literature reviews and background sections are definitional. In these sections, introducing the scholar before the example is appropriate. The rule applies to argument sections where scholars are being put to work, not introduced.
