# PR-03 — Short sentence for emphasis

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Use short sentences — including fragments — to land emphasis, create contrast, or signal a turn. A short sentence after a long one hits harder. A fragment at paragraph's end can close a thought with force that a full sentence cannot. Use this tool sparingly: if every third sentence is short, the rhythm becomes a tic.

---

## Diagnostic

Scan for where the argument pivots or arrives at a conclusion. Is the pivot sentence as long as the sentences around it? Shorten it.

---

## Examples

**With fragment:**
> The ranking algorithm sorts by recency. The moderation system flags by volume. The data pipeline logs everything. Three mechanisms. None visible to the user.

**Short after long:**
> Reddit's transparency reports show compliance rates rising from 88% in 2012 to 96% in 2022, a pattern that predates the platform's public statements about privacy and contradicts its self-presentation as a user-first platform. The record doesn't lie.

**Short for turn:**
> All of this suggests that cloze norming captures something real about reading. But the question was never whether it works. It was whether it works the same way a language model does.

---

## When not to use

Don't use fragments to assert things that need to be argued. "Correlation is not causation." belongs in a methodology section with support, not floating as an emphasis fragment. The short sentence earns its brevity; it doesn't use brevity to avoid the argument.
