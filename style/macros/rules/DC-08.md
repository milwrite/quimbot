# DC-08 — No gap-bridging constructions

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (required for academic profiles)

---

## Rule

Ban "the gap between X and Y," "bridges the gap," "this paper bridges," "fills the gap between." These constructions announce the paper's contribution by describing the distance it crosses, not what it does. State what the paper does instead.

---

## Why

Gap-bridging is the default move of a paper that doesn't know its own contribution. "This paper bridges the gap between reading research and NLP" tells the reader nothing about the argument, the methods, or the findings. It names the gap, not the bridge.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> This paper fills a gap in the literature by examining cloze probability alongside LLM surprisal.

> There is a gap between how reading researchers and computational linguists treat predictability, and this paper bridges it.

**Good:**
> This paper examines whether cloze probability and LLM surprisal measure the same cognitive process — and argues they do not.

> Reading researchers and computational linguists have treated predictability as equivalent across methods. This paper shows where that equivalence breaks down.

---

## What to write instead

State the paper's argument. State the paper's method. State what the paper finds. The contribution is implicit in all three. No gap needs to be named.
