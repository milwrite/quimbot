# VB-03 — Authors as agents

**Category:** Verb  
**Added:** 2026-03-21  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (academic profiles)

---

## Rule

Put researchers in the grammatical subject position. Don't nominalize their work into "the finding that X" or "the result that Y."

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> The finding that LLM-derived predictability outperforms cloze norming doesn't contradict the paper's argument.

**Good:**
> Veldre et al. found that LLM-enabled inference outperformed human cloze responses in fitting eye-movement data.

---

## Verb inventory

Researchers: found, showed, measured, reported, demonstrated, argued, proposed, traced, identified, tested, compared, noted

**Do not use:**
- "The study shows that..." (the study doesn't show; the researchers do)
- "The finding reveals..." (findings don't reveal; the researchers found)
- "The data suggests..." (acceptable only when you mean the dataset's statistical pattern, not as a passive way to avoid naming an author)

---

## Edge case: passive for institutional agents

"Users were compelled to hand over data" — acceptable when the institutional agent's role should be foregrounded or is genuinely diffuse. "Reddit handed over data" — preferred when you want to name the actor. Choose based on where you want the reader's attention.
