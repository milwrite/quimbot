# PR-05 — No consecutive simple independent clauses

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Three or more short sentences in a row with simple subject-verb structure reads as a list, not prose. Restructure by subordinating, embedding, or combining at least one into the chain.

---

## Diagnostic

Read five consecutive sentences. Are three or more of them simple subject-verb with no subordinate clauses? Combine or restructure.

---

## Examples

**Bad — list cadence:**
> Reddit cooperated with law enforcement. The compliance rate rose over time. Voluntary cooperation became standard. SESTA/FOSTA didn't change the pattern. It was already established.

**Good — syntactic complexity restored:**
> Reddit cooperated with law enforcement long before SESTA/FOSTA required it. Over the decade covered by the transparency reports, compliance rates rose steadily — voluntary cooperation had become standard practice well before the legal framework caught up.

---

## The target structure

Earn sentence length through subordinate clauses, participial phrases, and embedded qualifications. When a sentence runs long on conjunctions ("and... and... and"), split or restructure. When a paragraph runs long on short sentences, combine by subordinating.

---

## Exception

Short sentences can cluster intentionally when the list rhythm is the rhetorical point — when enumeration signals that each item stands separately and equally. This is rare in academic prose. In general prose or microblogs it appears more legitimately. When in doubt: add syntactic complexity.
