# SX-01 — Interrupted subject

**Category:** Syntax  
**Added:** 2026-03-20  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (all profiles)

---

## Rule

No sentences where the subject is followed by a comma-bracketed phrase before the verb arrives.

**Pattern to ban:** Subject, [qualifier], verb.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> The second debate, equally consequential for what the procedure actually measures, concerned deletion method.

> Taylor, working within the Gestalt tradition, developed the cloze procedure.

**Good:**
> The second debate concerned deletion method and carried equal weight for what the procedure actually measures.

> Taylor developed the cloze procedure within the Gestalt tradition.

---

## Fixes

1. Move the qualifier after the verb
2. Make it a separate sentence
3. Rewrite as a subordinate clause before the subject

---

## Exception

Very short, clearly non-restrictive appositives ("Oller, a linguist, argued...") are acceptable if:
- The phrase is two or three words
- The verb is not buried

When in doubt, restructure.
