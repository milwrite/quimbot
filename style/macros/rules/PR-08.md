# PR-08 — Tricolon limit

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06 (from STYLE_GUIDE.md 2026-03-19)  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

One tricolon per argument cluster. Multiple tricolons back-to-back signal pattern-recognition failure, not rhetorical control. Extended lists of four or five items that mimic tricolon form have the same problem — cut to three or restructure.

---

## Diagnostic

Scan a paragraph for lists of three that use parallel structure. If there are two or more in the same paragraph, keep the strongest; restructure or cut the others.

---

## Examples

**Bad — tricolon stacking:**
> Products impress people; platforms empower them. Products solve problems; platforms create worlds. Products scale linearly; platforms scale exponentially.

Three tricolon constructions back-to-back. The rhetorical power of any one is canceled by the repetition.

**Good — one tricolon, varied structure elsewhere:**
> Platforms don't solve problems; they create worlds. Unlike products, which impress and scale, platforms alter the conditions under which users build their own economies of participation.

---

## Why the rule exists

A tricolon works because the third element lands with emphasis — the reader registers it as a completion. Three tricolons in a row tell the reader to expect tricolons and the emphasis evaporates. The form becomes a tic.
