# SX-03 — Bridging construction

**Category:** Syntax  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Kill constructions that delay the real subject by announcing it first: "is what X," "is this:," "is that," "what X shows is," "what emerges is." These push the meaningful subject into a predicate slot and weaken the sentence's logical claim.

---

## Diagnostic

Does the sentence begin with "What" + verb + "is"? Does the sentence use a colon as a structural hinge where the main clause belongs? Cut the announcement and lead with the thing.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> What the compliance record reveals is a pattern of cooperation that predates SESTA/FOSTA.

> The result is this: participation trajectories diverge along the same axes as infrastructure decisions.

> What emerges from the data is a picture of voluntary cooperation.

**Good:**
> The compliance record reveals cooperation that predates SESTA/FOSTA.

> Participation trajectories diverge along the same axes as infrastructure decisions.

> The data shows voluntary cooperation as the dominant pattern.

---

## Fixes

1. Delete the announcement phrase; let the verb carry the meaning
2. Restructure so the meaningful agent is the subject
3. If a colon is introducing a list or formal definition, it may stay; if it's a rhetorical pause or hinge, remove it
