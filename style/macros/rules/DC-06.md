# DC-06 — No weak expletive openers

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

Ban "There is/are/was/were" as sentence openers. Name the thing instead of announcing its existence.

---

## Why

Expletive constructions ("There is," "There are") defer the real subject to the end of the sentence. They waste the subject slot — the strongest position — on a grammatical placeholder. The thing being named always has more force than "there."

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> There is no long-range interaction in this model.

> There are three mechanisms that shape participation: ranking, retention, and logging.

> There was a significant increase in compliance between 2015 and 2020.

**Good:**
> The model has no long-range interaction.

> Three mechanisms shape participation: ranking, retention, and logging.

> Compliance increased significantly between 2015 and 2020.

---

## Fixes

1. Identify the real subject (what exists, what acts)
2. Move it to the front
3. Give it a verb that does something

---

## Exception

"There are two possibilities" is acceptable as a structural signal at the start of a paragraph that will enumerate those possibilities. Expletives as deliberate list-setups are acceptable when the list is the point. The ban targets expletives that displace a real subject from its natural position.
