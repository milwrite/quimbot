# AK-02 — Don't fit the frame to the subject

**Category:** Academic register  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (required for academic profiles)

---

## Rule

Don't force a theoretical frame onto a subject when the frame's vocabulary doesn't actually name what the subject experiences. Check that abstractions fit the actual subject position: "legal risk" belongs to lawyers calculating exposure; CUNY students face "vulnerability" or being "findable." Use the term that names their actual condition.

---

## Why

When writers reach for a theoretical frame before they understand the subject, the frame overrides the subject's actual conditions. "Extraction pressures" is a platform-studies analyst's category; Reddit users face data logging, behavioral tracking, and the possibility of subpoenas. Name what the subject actually encounters.

---

## Diagnostic

For every abstract noun or theoretical term applied to a specific population or situation: ask — would a member of that population use this term to describe their experience? If not, is there a more precise term that names what they actually face?

---

## Examples

**Bad — frame misfit:**
> CUNY students face significant legal risk through their platform participation.

> Users experience extraction pressures from the platform's data architecture.

**Good — subject-fitted:**
> CUNY students risk becoming findable through their public Reddit posts — identifiable by employers, institutions, or law enforcement without their knowledge.

> The platform retains behavioral logs that users cannot access or delete, creating a record that persists beyond the content itself.

---

## The test

Substitute a specific person — "a student in a writing class at CUNY" — for the abstract subject. Does the sentence still make sense? Does it name something that student would recognize as their situation? If not, revise.
