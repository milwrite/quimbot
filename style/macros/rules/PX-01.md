# PX-01 — Em dash

**Category:** Punctuation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (all profiles)

---

## Rule

A lone em dash as pivot, connector, or sentence-closer is banned. A paired em dash wrapping a genuine parenthetical aside is permitted. Test: close the dash pair and restore the sentence — if the main clause still holds, the aside is legitimate. If not, restructure.

---

## Diagnostic

Count em dashes. Is there one? If it sits mid-sentence or at the end, it's almost certainly acting as a pivot or connector and must be removed. If there are two, apply the closure test.

---

## Examples

**Banned — pivot:**
> The forums work because they persist — that persistence is what infrastructure provides.

> The cloze procedure has a long history — one that spans behavioral and computational traditions.

**Banned — connector (after list):**
> Ranking, retention, data logging — these shape every interaction.

**Banned — sentence-closer:**
> The compliance record shows something significant — cooperation that predates SESTA/FOSTA.

**Permitted — genuine parenthetical aside:**
> The cloze procedure — developed by Taylor in 1953 — generates normed completion probabilities.

Test: "The cloze procedure generates normed completion probabilities." ✓ Main clause holds. Aside is legitimate.

---

## Fixes for banned uses

| Pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| X — Y (pivot) | Use a period or a subordinating conjunction |
| X — Y (connector) | Use a colon if Y is a list/definition; otherwise restructure |
| List — conclusion | Use a period. State the conclusion as a new sentence. |
| X — Y (sentence-closer) | Restructure: put the appositive before or after with a comma, or make it a separate sentence |
