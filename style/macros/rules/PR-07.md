# PR-07 — No hanging quotations

**Category:** Prose rhythm  
**Added:** 2026-04-06 (from STYLE_GUIDE.md 2026-03-22)  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

A quotation that occupies its own sentence without a grammatical host is banned. Every quote must be embedded in a clause that names who said it, what it does, or how it functions. A standalone quoted sentence used as a paragraph break or transition must be integrated.

---

## Test

Remove the quotation. Do the surrounding prose sentences parse as complete sentences? If removing the quote leaves nothing, the quote is hanging. Embed it as an appositive, a direct object, or a named formula.

---

## Examples

**Bad — hanging quotation:**
> ...the words they characteristically appear alongside. "You shall know a word by the company it keeps."

> ...a claim he compressed into a memorable formula. "You shall know a word by the company it keeps."

**Good — embedded:**
> ...or the words they characteristically appear alongside — a claim Firth compressed into the formula "you shall know a word by the company it keeps."

> Firth captured the distributional argument in a single line: "you shall know a word by the company it keeps."

---

## Integration options

1. **Appositive:** "...a claim he named [term], 'quotation'"
2. **Direct object:** "Firth wrote that 'quotation'"
3. **Named formula:** "...which Firth compressed into the formula 'quotation'"
4. **Attribution phrase:** "As Firth observed, 'quotation'"

The attribution phrase is the weakest option — use when the other forms would distort the syntax.
