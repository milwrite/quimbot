# PX-02 — Colon pivot

**Category:** Punctuation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (strong revision habit)

---

## Rule

Colons introduce lists and formal definitions only. Never use a colon as a structural hinge, rhetorical pause, or em-dash replacement. If a colon precedes a single clause rather than a list or named term, restructure so the syntax carries the relationship.

---

## Diagnostic

What follows the colon? If it's a list or a formally named term/definition, the colon is correct. If it's a single clause that continues or explains the preceding clause, the colon is acting as a hinge and must go.

---

## Examples

**Banned — rhetorical hinge:**
> The compliance record reveals one thing: Reddit cooperated before the law required it.

> The problem is clear: participation trajectories encode infrastructure pressures.

> There is only one conclusion: the functional asymmetry claim holds.

**Permitted — formal list:**
> Three rules govern this: (1) no interrupted subject, (2) no nominalized opener, (3) no colon pivot.

**Permitted — formal definition:**
> The term used throughout is "infrastructure": systems that become visible upon breakdown.

---

## Fixes

| Pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| "X: Y (single clause)" | Make two sentences; or use a conjunction; or embed Y as a subordinate clause |
| "The result: Y" | "The result was Y" or "Y followed" |
| "The point: Y" | Cut the announcement; write Y directly |
