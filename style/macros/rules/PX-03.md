# PX-03 — Punctuation stacking

**Category:** Punctuation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

No consecutive sentences that each use heavy punctuation (em dashes, colons, semicolons) as their primary connective tissue. These marks lose their force when stacked. Mix punctuation weight across a paragraph.

---

## Diagnostic

Read three consecutive sentences. Do two or more use the same heavy mark? Does the rhythm feel mechanical — pause, pause, pause? Vary it.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> The ranking algorithm sorts by recency; the moderation system flags by volume; the data pipeline logs everything. Reddit cooperated with law enforcement — that cooperation predates SESTA/FOSTA. The record shows one thing: the platform chose compliance long before the law required it.

Three different heavy marks in three sentences, each doing the same job of connecting a main clause to a secondary one.

**Good:**
> The ranking algorithm sorts by recency, the moderation system flags by volume, and the data pipeline logs everything. Reddit cooperated with law enforcement long before SESTA/FOSTA required it. The compliance record confirms this pattern: the platform chose cooperation.

---

## Fixes

1. Identify which mark carries the most important relationship; keep that one
2. Convert the other connections to subordinate clauses, coordinating conjunctions, or separate sentences
3. Use the weight of sentence length to vary rhythm instead

---

## Note

This rule applies to paragraph-level rhythm, not single sentences. A single sentence with a semicolon is fine. Three consecutive sentences with semicolons each is the problem.
