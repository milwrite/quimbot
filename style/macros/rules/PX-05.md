# PX-05 — Single separator rule

**Category:** Punctuation  
**Added:** 2026-04-06 (from STYLE_GUIDE.md 2026-03-29)  
**Non-negotiable:** No (revision habit)

---

## Rule

A clause should use at most one structural separator: colon, semicolon, or enclosing em-dash pair. Two separators in one clause means two logical relationships have been papered over. Split the clause and surface both connectors explicitly.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> She finished the report — which took three weeks — and, crucially, submitted it on time: ahead of the deadline.

> The compliance record shows cooperation — consistent, documented cooperation — across five years: a pattern that predates the law.

**Good:**
Split into two or three sentences, each with its own explicit connector:
> She finished the report, which took three weeks. She submitted it ahead of the deadline.

> The compliance record shows consistent, documented cooperation across five years. This pattern predates the law.

---

## Why

Stacking separators signals a writer avoiding the work of articulating what each relationship actually is. Each separator papers over a logical connection. Two separators mean two unexamined connections. Surface both.
