# AK-01 — Concrete before abstract

**Category:** Academic register  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** No (required for academic profiles)

---

## Rule

In academic prose, give the example, event, or name first. Let the inference or theoretical frame follow. Never lead with the abstraction and use the example as illustration.

---

## Why

Readers cannot evaluate an abstract claim they haven't yet seen grounded. The example earns the theory; the theory explains the example. Reverse this order and the reader either accepts the claim on authority or holds it in suspension until evidence arrives — neither of which builds an argument.

---

## Examples

**Bad — abstract first:**
> Infrastructure becomes visible upon breakdown. Reddit's content moderation policies illustrate this.

**Good — concrete first:**
> In 2015, Reddit banned r/fatpeoplehate and several other subreddits under a new content policy. The change immediately surfaced the platform's governance structure for users who had previously treated community rules as natural features of the interface. Star and Ruhleder would call this visibility upon breakdown infrastructure.

---

## The pattern

1. Name the specific event, dataset, platform behavior, or textual example
2. Show what it does — what it reveals, produces, or constrains
3. Name the theoretical frame as a description of what the reader just saw

---

## Exception

When the theoretical term is being defined for the first time (in a literature review or background section), leading with the term is acceptable. The rule applies to argument sections where the theory is being put to work, not to definitional sections.
