# DC-07 — No contrastive pivots

**Category:** Diction  
**Added:** 2026-04-06  
**Non-negotiable:** Yes (all profiles)

---

## Rule

Kill all contrastive constructions: "not X but Y," "not just X," "not only X but also Y," "less about X, more about Y," "beyond X," "more than X." State the point directly. This applies to single sentences, paragraph topic sentences, and section framings.

---

## The pattern

Every contrastive pivot has the real claim buried in its second half. Write the second half. Cut the first.

---

## Examples

**Bad:**
> This is not a paper about reading — it is a paper about the tools we use to study reading.

> The cloze task is not merely a proxy measure but a direct window into contextual constraint.

> Platform governance is not about content moderation alone but about the full architecture of participation.

**Good:**
> This paper studies the tools used to study reading.

> The cloze task directly measures contextual constraint.

> Platform governance encompasses the full architecture of participation.

---

## Why this is non-negotiable

Contrastive constructions signal hedging. "Not just X, but Y" suggests the writer expects the reader to assume X and needs to correct them. Most of the time the reader wasn't assuming X. The framing creates a problem it then solves, where no problem existed.

The deeper issue: contrastive constructions are often used to smuggle in an implicit claim about what the reader thinks. Never frame an argument around what the reader supposedly assumed unless you have evidence they assumed it.
